The "Adjust-A-Mirror"

Tackling the problem of self-catherization

This project started because I wanted to make a pen that felt good, designed and built well enough so that I wouldn't need another.

A model of my my pen, exploded to see the bolt and pin

Research

I started with research and measurement. Which ink should I use, what should my bolt and clip be made of, what kind of springs do I want, and finally, how am I actually going to machine it.

I ended up with quite the list. Most operations I would do on the lathe, but the facing to accommodate the clip as well as the oddly shaped slot for the pin would have to be milled out.

Then I actually went about trying to machine it, which wasn’t easy! I had to learn a few new specialized tools as well as improve my overall fundamentals on the mill and lathe. The product you see imaged here is actually version 3 of my pen!

Pleased with my results, I went on to make another for my beloved mother, this time out of copper. Love you mom :)
